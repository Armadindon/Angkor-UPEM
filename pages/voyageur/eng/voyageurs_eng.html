    • Louis Delaporte (1842-1925)

    Who was he ?

    French explorer of the 19th century, it was very young that he decided to become a sailor. To make this aspiration a reality, he joined the Naval School in Brest in 1858. Appointed midshipman two years later, he embarked on his first expedition to Mexico.

    Why is he related to Angkor ?

    Recruited in 1866 for the French Expedition in the Mekong (mission to explore and search for the sources of the river), it was on this occasion that he discovered the Angkor site. The discovery of these forgotten ruins deeply upset Delaporte and he decided to dedicate his life to making this civilization known, which he compared to the greatness of the ancient Egyptian civilization. Having become passionate about Angkor since his exhibition in Cambodia, he brought back a large variety of objects of the Khmer empire from his various expeditions (1866 to 1882). At the end of his career, as he could no longer go on expeditions, he devoted his time to the Khmer Museum in Compiègne, where he worked as a curator.

    Quote :

    "I could not contemplate these monuments... without feeling the strong desire to make them known to Europe and to enrich our museums with a collection of Khmer antiquities whose place belong next to those of Egypt and Assyria." Voyage au Cambodge, Paris, 1880, Éditions Delagrave.



    Some of his works related to the Angkor site :

    • Voyage d'exploration en Indo-Chine, Paris 1873, with Francis Garnier.
    • Voyage au Cambodge, Paris, 1880, Éditions Delagrave [archive].Book republished in 1999 by Maisonneuve and Larose editions.
    • Les monuments du Cambodge, Paris 1924 (2 vol.).
    Visited temple :
    • Banteay Srei


    André Malraux (1901-1976)

    Who was he ?

    Writer, politician and adventurer, he is considered as one of the greatest consciences of the 20th century.

    Why is he related to Angkor ?

    On October 13, 1923, André Malraux left for Siem Reap with his wife, Clara Malraux. Nevertheless, it was not just as a tourist but to steal bas-reliefs from Angkor and sell them in the West to make up for his lack of money. Once there, Malraux and his expedition team detached nearly a ton of carved stones and four large pieces of bas-reliefs. Back in Angkor, they were reported to the police, who sentenced them to three years in prison. His sentence will then be reduced to one year and eight months of suspended jail.

    Some of his works related to the Angkor site :

    • La voie Royale, 1930, Grasset edition

    Temple(s) visité(s) :

    Tous

    Quote :

    « All adventurers are born of a liar » Malraux dans “La voie Royale”, 1930, Edition Grasset



    Henri Mouhot (1826-1861)

    Who was he ?

    French explorer and naturalist. After reading Sir John Bowring's (1857) The Kingdom and People of Siam: with a narrative of a mission to that country in 1855, he decided to discover Siam, Cambodia and Laos. During his expedition to Cambodia he met and became a friend of the kings of Cambodia Ang Duong, who ruled until 1860, and then of Norodom. During the years 1859 - 1860, he explored and had the Angkor site rediscovered in the eyes of the West.

    Quote :

    "Instead of being disappointed, as you approach, you experience a deeper admiration and pleasure. First of all, they are beautiful and tall square columns, all in one piece; porticoes, capitals, rounded roofs in domes; all built in large blocks beautifully polished, carved and sculpted." End of Chapter 17 of Voyage dans les royaumes de Siam, de Cambodge, de Laos et autres parties centrales de l'Indo-Chine, Henri Mouhot.

    Some of his works related to the Angkor site :

    Voyage dans les royaumes de Siam, de Cambodge, de Laos et autres parties centrales de l'Indo-Chine, L. Hachette (Paris), 1868 , Henri Mouhot.

    Visited temple :

    All of them.



    Pierre Loti (1850-1923)

    Who was he ?

    Novelist and Naval Officer. As a child, Pierre Loti dreamed of seeing the city of Angkor. It was only long after that he realized his dream when he travelled around the world as a sailor. In 1900, he was assigned to the battleship "Redoutable" and it was during a stopover in 1901 that he moored on the Cambodian coast.

    Quote :

    "These colossal enclosures and its towers, which have just appeared to us like a mirage of the torrid heat, are not the same city, but only Angkor-Vat, its main temple. The city, Angkor Thom, we hear that it is located further, immense and imprecise, buried under the tropical forest." Le pèlerin d'Angkor, Pierre Loti.













    Zhou Daguan 周达观 (1266-1346)

    Who was he ?

    Zhou Daguan 周达观 is a Chinese diplomat, geographer and writer working for the Emperor ChangZong of the Yuan (元成宗).

    Why is he related to Angkor ?

    Zhou Daguan was part of a Chinese embassy sent to Angkorian Cambodia by the Emperor Changzong of the Yuan (元成宗) in 1295. Zhou Daguan stayed in Angkor for a year, when he returned he dedicated a book to all aspects of Cambodia at the time. Inside, forty chapters are devoted to the monarchy, the nobility, civil servants, the small people, housing, food, agriculture, the seasons, writing, language and even clothing. This book is therefore a complete and reliable description of what was then the largest empire of Southeast Asia.

    Some of his works related to the Angkor site :

    Zhou Daguan and Paul Pelliot, Mémoires sur les coutumes du Cambodge de Tcheou Ta-Kouan, vol. 3, Adrien Maisonneuve, coll. « Œuvres posthumes » Paul Pelliot translated Zhou Daguan's books - Mémoires sur les coutumes du Cambodge

    Visited temple :

    • Bayon
    • Baphuon
    • Angkor Vat
    • The Elephant Terrace


    Paul Claudel (1868-1955)

    Who was he ?

    Paul Claudel was a French playwright, poet, essayist and diplomat. After the Foreign Affairs competitive exam, he became France's ambassador of Japan. Elected at the French Academy in 1946, he devoted the rest of his life to the study of biblical texts.

    Why is he related to Angkor ?

    On his way to Japan, he went through Cambodia and more precisely through Angkor. In his diary, he shares his feelings about the temples. For him, evil springs from all over Angkor, the five towers of Angkor Wat are compared to blasphemous pineapples.

    Quote :

    "" Angkor is indeed one of the most cursed, evil places I know. I had come back sick and the relationship I had made from my trip perished in a fire." (Cahier V, 1925)

    Some of his works related to the Angkor site :

    Journal, Cahier IV, octobre 1921

    Visited temple :

    • Ta Prohm
    • Ta Keo
    • The Gate of Victory
    • le Bayon
    • Angkor Vat


    Lucien Fournereau (1846-1906)

    Who was he ?

    Michel Louis Lucien Fournereau is a French explorer and archaeologist. From 1886 to 1888 he was in charge of several archaeological expeditions in Indochina.

    Why is he related to Angkor?

    During his expedition to Indochina, he specialized in the Siem Reap area and studied in detail the ancient town of Angkor Thom. He also took many photos of the site and made casts of sculptures for the Trocadero Museum.

    Some of his works related to the Angkor site :

    • Les Ruines khmères du Cambodge siamois, Bulletin de la Société de géographie, vol.II, 1889, p. 242
    • Les Ruines d'Angkor, étude artistique et historique, 1890
    • Les Ruines Khmères, Cambodge et Siam, 1890
    • Bangkok, Le Tour du monde, vol.II, 1894, p. 349-397
    • Le Siam ancien, archéologie, épigraphie, géographie, 2 vol, Annales du Musée Guimet XXVII et XXXI, 1895-1908

    Visited temple :

    All of them.



    Georges Groslier( 1887 - 1945 )

    Who was he ?

    Protector of the arts, scientist, writer, ethnologist, novelist, photographer and artist.

    Why is he related to Angkor ?

    Born in Cambodia, and son of an administrator of the Civil Services of Indochina, he studied painting at the School of Fine Arts of Paris. Disappointed by a second Grand Prix of Rome, he preferred to join his family and discovered Angkor. Back in France, he multiplied numerous conferences and publication to introduce Khmer art.

    Some of his works related to the Angkor site :

    • A l’Ombre d’Angkor, Georges Groslier (1915)

    All of them.
