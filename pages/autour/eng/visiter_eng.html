What to visit

The Cambodgian is overflowing of places to visit.

Here is a list of things to do in Cambodgia.

Sbek Thom, a Khmer shadow theater

Registered in the Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity in 2008, this Khmer theatre features non-articulated puppets, made of carved leather, sometimes reaching a height of 2m. Dating from the pre-Angkorian period and considered a sacred art, these puppet performances were once dedicated to the deities, and they were only given three or four times a year. After the fall of Angkor in the fifteenth century, shadow theatre went beyond ritual to become an artistic form, but without losing its ceremonial dimension. Representations are usually taking place at night, outdoors, near a scribble or a pagoda. A white sheet is tightened with two bamboo sticks in front of lights. The shapes of the dolls are projected in chinese shadow on a white screen. The artist is giving life to these shadows by doing some dance steps, an orchestra and two narrators are supporting the dramatic action. These representations can last for many consecutive nights and use 160 dolls for the same show.

The Royal Cambodian Ballet

Registered in the Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity in 2008, this Khmer art is renowned for the gracious gestures and gorgeous outfits. More than just a simple dance, this art is part of the history and the identity of the Cambodian people. This art form, which narrowly escaped annihilation in the 1970s, is in fact revered by many Cambodians. Dance, with its sacred and symbolic role, embodies the traditional values of refinement, respect and spirituality. His repertory immortalizes the founding legends of the Khmer people. That is why Cambodians have always considered it as the emblem of Khmer culture. The classical repertoire includes four types of characters: Neang the woman, Neayrong the man, Yeak the giant and Sva the monkey. Each one has its own colours, costumes, make-up and masks. The gestures and postures, which require years of intensive training to master, reflect the full range of human emotions, from fear and rage to love and joy. An orchestra accompanies the dance, while a women's choir comments on the plot and highlights the emotions mimicked by the dancers.

For a relaxing evening, take a walk through the streets of Pub Street between pubs, restaurant and shop. You can attend traditional dance performances or test the fish pedicure.

The Angkor National Museum

For the more educated, enjoy a visit to the Angkor National Museum. You will discover 1500 years of history from the establishment of the Funan kingdom in the 1st century AD, until the fall of the great Khmer empire. The museum is composed of different galleries retracing the golden age of the Khmer Empire:

  • Gallery A - The Khmer civilization

    Discover the reason that led the Khmer civilization to create one of the largest architectural structures in the world. Explore the beliefs of these Khmer people towards their great Kings and learn about the history of the ancient war that ravaged the lands of Cambodia.

  • Gallery B - Religion and beliefs/h4> Get to know the ancient Khmer people through their religion and beliefs that shaped Khmer culture, as well as their literary, sculptural, architectural and everyday life.

  • Gallery C - The great Khmer Kings

    Learn more about the major Khmer Kings who made the Khmer Empire one of the greatest ancient civilizations in the world.

  • Gallery D - Angkor Wat

    Find descriptions on the famous Angkor Wat temple.

  • Gallery E - Angkor Thom

  • Gallery F - History through stones

    Trace back through the inscriptions on the stones, the evidence that one day the Khmer Empire existed.

  • Gallery G - The ancient traditions

    Discover the fascinating Khmer customs like the Apsara dance.
