Angkor Vat

Date : 12th century

Description : The Angkor Wat Temple (ប្រាសាទអង្គរវត្គរវត្គរវត) was initially dedicated to the god Vishnu before becoming a Buddhist temple. It was built at the behest of King Suryavarman II from 1113 to about 1150, and it combines two Khmer architectural techniques: Mountain temple and temple with galleries. It contains 4 enclosure facing West:

  1. Enclosure I on the third floor features a two-storey basement with three staircases per face, an interior gallery with windows on the outside, a portico, corner towers, four gopuras (a type of Hindu architecture, representing a structure through which one enters the successive enclosures of a temple) and passages leading to the central tower
  2. Enclosure II, on the second floor, has a gopura with three entrances to the west. The various gopuras are connected to the corner towers by galleries with windows on the inside.
  3. Enclosure III, on the first floor, has a large gallery decorated with bas-reliefs, open to the outside world, as well as four corner pavilions and four entrance pavilions, which are representations of Hindu mythological stories (Ramayana, the churning of the sea of milk, the epic Mahabharata story or the devata or Apsaras).
  4. Enclosure IV consists of a laterite wall and surrounded by a moat, the central part of the western gopura has three entrances and is framed by galleries, there are also two libraries.
  5. Traces of color can still be seen on the temple walls today, which confirms that Angkor Wat was once covered with colours, some researchers say that the wall were covered with white paint and the roof were golden.

Materials : sandstone, laterite

Angkor Thom

Date : 12th century

Description : Angkor Thom (អង្គរធំ) has a wall that forms a 3 km square with a surface area of 900 hectares, approximately 8 m high, built laterite and bordered on the outside by a 100 m wide moat. This wall is interrupted by five monumental gates (face towers) and bordered by a balustrade of statues of giants carrying the snake body.

Materials :sandstone (doors), laterite (enclosure)

Ta Prohm

Date : 1186

Description : Ta Prohm (Khmer: ប្រាសាទតាព្រហ្ម, originally called រាជវិហារ, Rājavihara) is a five-enclosure temple:

  1. The first enclosure is a gallery with gopura (a type of Hindu architecture, representing a structure through which one enters the successive enclosures of a temple) axial and corner pavilions. The central tower is connected by covered structures to gopura I East and I West.
  2. The second enclosure consists of a laterite wall with an inner portico, as well as multiple openings in the east side.
  3. The third enclosure consists of a gallery open to the outside with gopura and corner pavilions.
  4. The fourth enclosure is distinguished by a laterite wall and four gopuras as well as by ruined brick cells with a moat
  5. The fifth enclosure is characterized by a laterite wall with ridges decorated with Buddha and four cardinal gopuras with face towers.

Materials : sandstone, laterite, brick

Bayon

Date : end of the 12th century

Description : Bayon (ប្រាសាទបាទបាយ័ន) is the latest of the "temple-mountains" of the three-enclosure Angkor site:

  1. The first enclosure has a tower with a face dedicated by Jayavarman VII to the Buddha, two "libraries" and a gallery passage.
  2. The second enclosure has numerous facial towers, two "libraries" and a cistern
  3. The third enclosure has a large central massif with a face, with four towers and six side buildings.

Materials : sandstone, laterite

Banteay Srei

Date : second half of the 10th century

Description : This temple consists of three enclosures

  1. The first speaker contains two small gopuras. The courtyard is occupied by three sanctuary towers open to the East, an axial pavilion and two "libraries".
  2. The second enclosure is occupied by a laterite stepped ditch basin that separates the wall of the second enclosure from that of the third and is intersected by a causeway leading to the east and west gopura.

Materials : sandstone, laterite, brick

Banteay Samré

Date : 12th century

Description : Banteay Samré (ប្រាសាទបនាទបន្ទាយសំរែ) is a temple with a central tower in the style of Angkor Wat. It is surrounded by two concentric walls containing two inner enclosures, 4 gopuras, a room and two libraries

Ta Prohm

Date : 12th century

Description : Banteay Kdei (ប្រាសាទបន្ទ្ទាយក្) is a temple with three inner and one outer enclosures:

  1. The first enclosure includes 4 gopuras with galleries connecting to the main sanctuary. The sanctuary is a square enclosure
  2. The second enclosure is part of the main temple with gopura built as a gallery with a wall and double row of pillars.
  3. The third enclosure is a gopura with a cruciform plan and an access to the main sanctuary as well as a "Dancers' Hall".
  4. The external enclosure has 4 gopura

Materials : sandstone, latérite

Phiméanakas (ou Palais Royal)

Date : fin Xème siècle , début XIème siècle

Description : Phiméanakas (ប្រាសាទភិមានអាកាស, “le temple céleste”) un ensemble clos dont le mur d’enceinte haut de 5 mètres est doublé d’un second mur ayant une date plus récente. A l’Est, on retrouve un vaste pavillon d’entrée et sur chacune de ses faces Nord et Sud deux pavillons d’entrée plus simple.
Au centre de cette enceinte se trouve le Phimeanakas, un temple-montagne à trois gradins, construit en latérite et très étroits, au sommet se trouve une galerie voûtée et une tour centrale en ruine construites toutes deux en grès

Matériaux : latérite, grès

Ta Som

Date : end of the 12th century

Description : This temple is composed of three enclosures:

  1. The first enclosure includes a 20 x 30 metre sanctuary tower. Inside, there is also a gallery, corner pavilions with crossed vaults and molded false doors and four gopuras. Two open libraries are located in the inner courtyard. The central sanctuary with four forebodies and openings on all four sides
  2. The second enclosure, whose laterite wall is cut off to the east and west by a gopura with a cruciform plan
  3. The third enclosure is formed by a laterite with gopuras with face towers.

Materials : grès, latérite

Lolei

Date : end of the 9th century

Description : This monument is built in the middle of the Baray de Lolei (Indratataka), with a large body of water. The temple is known for its decorative elements (blind doors, devata and Dvarapala, the god of heaven Indra or Vishnu). Lolei consists of four brick towers grouped on a two-storey laterite terrace, one of which collapsed in the 1960s.

Materials : laterite, sandstone

Phnom Bakheng (Yaśodhragiri)

Date : end of the 9th century, beginning of the 10th century

Description : Phnom Bakheng (ប្រាសាទភាទភ្នំបំបាខែង) a mountain temple built on the top of a hill with axial stairs. The pyramid has 5 tiers and has staggered towers at its top on a common terrace. There are small towers on the steps of the pyramid, the base is surrounded by bricks. There are also two "libraries" located to the east. The four axial gopuras and the laterite enclosure are ruined.

Materials : sandstone, bricks, laterite

X

Elephant Terrace

Date : 13th century

Description : The Elephant Terrace (ព្រះលានជលានជល់ដំរំរី) is located in front of the Royal Palace, elongated North-South with three major and two secondary steps to the east. It is a ceremonial terrace decorated with wooden structures that have now disappeared. Its name comes from the elephants in very cut-out high reliefs decorating the secondary stairs. The east side of the retaining wall is decorated with representations of elephants and garuda.

Materials : laterite

Ta Keo

Date : No known date

Description : Ta Keo (ប្រាសែវាទតាកែវ) is the first temple built exclusively in sandstone, with 5 enclosures :

  1. the first enclosure has two long galleries with wooden and tiled roofs, with laterite-based windows and sandstone wall
  2. the second enclosure contains a gopura with four cardinal points containing three footbridges and a central tower
  3. the third enclosure has an internal gallery and is purely decorative
  4. the fourth enclosure has a terrace and two "libraries".
  5. the fifth enclosure has the central tower with four stairs leading to the top

Materials : sandstone

Leper King's Terrace (Preah Lean Sdach Kumlung)

Date : 13th century

Description : The Leprosy King's Terrace (ព្រះលានសានស្តេចគមេចគម្លង់) is located to the north of the Elephants' Terrace and consists of a masonry massif about 25 metres wide and 6 metres high. Its faces are entirely carved with characters spread over 7 registers representing the Hindu pantheon, including nāgas (snake), garuḍaset (eagle) and kumbhandás.

Materials : sandstone

Thommanon

Date : 12th century

Description : Thommanon (ប្រាសាទធមាទធម្មនន្) is a single enclosure temple facing east. The central group includes a false-storey tower and front building connected by a covered passage to an elongated pavilion. The laterite enclosure is completely destroyed and is bordered on the outside by a moat

Materials : laterite

Bakong

Date : end of the 9th century

Description : The temple is composed of a five-storey pyramid surrounded by three enclosures:

  1. The first enclosure forms a large rectangle (160m * 120m) and includes four cruciform gopura. It is in the centre of this enclosure that the five-storey pyramid is located, the fourth gate of which carries twelve small sandstone towers. At the top is a sandstone tower and around the pyramid are eight brick towers and various buildings.
  2. The wall of the second enclosure is cut off to the west and east by cruciform gopura
  3. The third enclosure houses many brick towers

Materials : laterite

X

PreahKhan

Date : second half of the 12th century

Description : This temple is a large complex forming a rectangle of 700 meters by 800 meters, it covers an area of 56 hectares. There are some similarities with Ta Prohm but Prah Khan has only four enclosure:

  1. The first enclosure is interrupted by four gopuras, inside there are a large number of galleries and sanctuaries. The main tower with a cruciform plan has four front bodies with numerous vaulted constructions.
  2. The second enclosure is an open gallery to the outside with six small towers aligned on the east side
  3. The third enclosure is cut off by four gopuras, the eastern one is particularly important as it houses the "Dancer's Hall", a column building and cloistered structures.
  4. The fourth enclosure has the particularity of having a gopura East with three towers. Inside is a stopover gîte. It is surrounded by a moat crossed by four giant carriageways preceded by bollard alleys.

Materials : sandstone, laterite
