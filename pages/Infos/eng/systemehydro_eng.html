Hydrolic system

The construction of these gigantic temples was far from easy. It was necessary to route the sandstone from a quarry located about forty kilometers, then erect these sanctuaries with complex architecture.

Stones often used : laterite, sandstone, brick

Sandstone : gray / pink stone from Phnom Kulhen quarry

Laterite: brown porous stone used for the bases of the structures

Stones : According to recent archaeological research, sandstone stone was transported through a network of canals from Angkor to the quarry. This result was revealed thanks to the presence of authigenic calcite in the stones which testifies to the presence of water. The sandstone blocks were probably routed through rafts along the canals.

Khmer owned neither cement nor mortar. Thus, to make the joints between the different blocks of stone, the Khmer rode the surface of the stones (rubbing stones between them to make the surfaces smooth) so that the blocks of stones rest on each other. This process is very efficient because two perfectly flat surfaces are difficult to dissociate. Nevertheless, the process is based only on the force of gravity and was therefore affected by nature (vegetation, water infiltration ...)
